Motherboards

The motherboard is the PCB that is the home of many of the vital components of a computor such as the CPU, CPU fans, intergrated graphics/graphics cards and the BIOS

Origins of the Motherboard

Before the creation of the motherboard, the components such as the CPU, the BIOS, the memory and the peripherals were placed on seperate printed circuit boards, making the PC much larger. In the late 1980's, many PC companies were making their PC's with the components on seperate PCB's, until Apple and IBM had chosen to place most of the required parts on the same PCB, hence the name motherboard (because that is the hub where all of the components are located) These leaders of innovation had made rough and ready motherboards, that many companies had copied and made slight changes to, leading to the reason that there are so many companies producing computers to date.

The Funtions of the Motherboard

As stated beforehand, the motherboard is the main hub for most of the required components of the computer. The components are:

  • Sockets (Microprocessors location)
  • RAM slots
  • A chipset
  • Flash ROM (SSD, BIOS)
  • Clock generator (syncronization of pc components)
  • Power connectors (distributes power to the cpu)
  • Connectors (Typically for hard drives)

The Uses of the Motherboard

The motherboard is, as stated many a times the most important part of a computer, as it is the host to the CPU. The motherboard takes the power from the outlet and distributes it around the computer to all of the main components such as the fans, the memory and the other components of the motherboard. The RAM used for the computer is used by the CPU, to make sure that the computer does not overclock and crash from running out of memory.
